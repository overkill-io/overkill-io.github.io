Part 2 - GT08 Teardown

Posted by arturo182 on Apr 3, 2017
In Project: Overwatch

In the previous part of this dev blog I mentioned that I chose the chinese GT08 smartwatch as the base for a DIY smartwatch project. In this part we’ll go through a teardown of one such watch and we’ll compare it with another GT08 that was acquired from a different source.

The casing

The GT08’s body is pretty uniform, the metal casing bounds the watch on both sides, with the pretty seamless LCD on the front and a plastic cover on the back, the cover doesn’t require any screws, it just clips on.

On the left side, the watch has a microphone hole on the top, a rubber plug hiding the micro USB in the middle and a speaker grill on the bottom. The rubber plug has a hole in it to make it easier to pull it out. This, in addition to the hole and grill, means the watch is not water tight, but it might survive a quick splash while washing ones hands. On the right side there is a single button, it looks like it might turn, but it is a simple button that only register pushes. Above the screen there is a round, transparent window that is used by the camera for taking watch-selfies, as I mentioned in Part 1, the plan is to put a ALS there, which will allow dynamic screen brightness adjustment.

The straps are not interchangeable with regular watch bands, but they can be removed. One of the straps has the GSM antenna inside it and while we won’t be using GSM in our replacement hardware, we could try to reuse the antenna so we can escape the metal casing and get better BLE range. Another option is to remove the straps and mount 3d printed adapter which would enable using regular watch straps. And while regular 43mm straps seem a little to narrow, it’s hard to asses at this point if wider straps are needed, but it is a future option.
